// beyond the code

Life Beyond Code

Software is my craft, but curiosity is my nature. When I step away from the terminal, you'll find me deep in an anime arc, filling sketchbooks with character art, chasing big ideas in books, or writing to understand the world a little better.

Anime

A few series that left a permanent mark — stories I'll carry for life.

Devilman Crybaby
Masaaki Yuasa's adaptation is brutal, surreal, and heartbreaking. A meditation on love and humanity at the end of the world. It stays with you.
Black Clover
Asta's relentless refusal to give up despite having no magic is one of the most motivating arcs in shonen. Pure heart over raw talent — every time.
Jujutsu Kaisen
Raw cursed energy and brutal fights wrapped around genuine character depth. Gojo Satoru is one of the most iconic characters modern anime has produced.
My Hero Academia
Deku's journey from quirkless kid to the greatest hero redefined what a shonen protagonist can be. All Might's debut still gives me chills.
Cyberpunk: Edgerunners
Studio Trigger at its absolute best. 10 episodes that hit harder than most 50-episode series. Lucy deserved the moon.
Demon Slayer
Ufotable set a new benchmark for animation quality. Tanjiro's kindness even toward demons makes this more than just a battle series.
One Piece
The greatest adventure ever told. Oda built a world so rich and alive that every arc feels like a different genre. The Straw Hats are family.
Naruto
The anime that started it all for me. Pain's arc and Naruto vs. Sasuke are childhood memories I'll never shake. Believe it.
Cowboy Bebop
Jazz, existentialism, and bounty hunters in space. Spike Spiegel is the coolest character ever drawn. See you space cowboy.
Attack on Titan
The narrative complexity of the final arc is unmatched in anime. Every twist recontextualises everything — a true masterpiece of storytelling.
Black Lagoon
Gritty, sharp, and unapologetically adult. Revy is one of anime's most complex female characters. A rare series that never glamorises its violence.

Reading

Books that permanently changed how I see the world — ones I keep coming back to.

DuneFrank Herbert
Politics, ecology, religion, and destiny woven into a desert world. The greatest science fiction novel ever written. The spice must flow.
Crime & PunishmentFyodor Dostoevsky
Raskolnikov's psychological spiral mirrors every moral question I've asked myself. Dostoevsky understood the human mind before psychology existed.
The Three-Body ProblemLiu Cixin
A sci-fi masterpiece that makes you feel cosmically small. The Dark Forest theory permanently changed how I think about civilisation.
SapiensYuval Noah Harari
After reading it, you see money, nations, religion, and AI as collective fictions we agreed to believe. Permanently perspective-shifting.
1984George Orwell
The surveillance state, doublethink, the memory hole. Orwell wrote a warning; we built it anyway. Still the most relevant novel ever written.
MeditationsMarcus Aurelius
A Roman emperor's private journal, never meant to be published. Two thousand years later, still the best manual for thinking under pressure.
FoundationIsaac Asimov
What if you could predict the fall of civilisation and build a shortcut through the dark ages? Asimov asks the biggest questions in the simplest prose.
Brave New WorldAldous Huxley
A dystopia built not on fear but on pleasure and comfort. Huxley's vision of control through happiness feels more accurate every year.
The Count of Monte CristoAlexandre Dumas
The ultimate revenge story. Dantès's transformation from prisoner to nobleman is one of literature's most satisfying arcs.
The Brothers KaramazovFyodor Dostoevsky
Faith, doubt, freedom, and the existence of God debated across a family falling apart. The most complete novel ever written — nothing is left unanswered.
The AlchemistPaulo Coelho
A short fable with a long echo. The idea that the universe conspires to help those who pursue their Personal Legend is one I return to often.
Atomic HabitsJames Clear
Small changes, remarkable results. The 1% improvement framework completely reshaped how I think about building skills and staying consistent.
Man's Search for MeaningViktor Frankl
Written by a Holocaust survivor who turned suffering into philosophy. If Frankl could find meaning there, the rest of us have no excuse not to try.
The StrangerAlbert Camus
Meursault's indifference to the world is both unsettling and oddly honest. Camus distilled absurdism into 120 pages of precise, cold prose.
Thus Spoke ZarathustraFriedrich Nietzsche
Dense, poetic, and challenging — Nietzsche's most ambitious work. The Übermensch concept and eternal recurrence rewired how I think about self-mastery.
NeuromancerWilliam Gibson
Gibson invented cyberspace before the internet existed. The aesthetic of this novel — neon, decay, and neural implants — shaped an entire century of sci-fi.
The RoadCormac McCarthy
A father and son walking through the end of the world. Bleak, relentless, and the most emotionally devastating novel I've ever read.
Flowers for AlgernonDaniel Keyes
Charlie Gordon's diary, as his intelligence grows then fades, is one of the most heartbreaking explorations of what it means to be human in all of literature.

Countries I've Visited

Five countries, each one reshaping how I see the world.

🇨🇦 Canada  1 month · Toronto
A month living in Toronto — the city's diversity is unlike anything I'd seen before. Every neighbourhood felt like a different country inside one city.
🇹🇳 Tunisia  7 visits · Multiple cities
Seven visits and it never gets old. The medinas, the sea, the food, the chaos of the souks — Tunisia is woven into who I am.
🇲🇦 Morocco  Visited · Marrakech
The colours of Marrakech, the labyrinthine medinas, mint tea and tajine. Morocco overwhelms all the senses at once — and I mean that in the best way.
🇬🇧 United Kingdom  Living here · Exeter
Home for university. Exeter is a medieval city studying the future — Roman walls, a 900-year-old cathedral, and a world-class CS department.
🇹🇷 Turkey  Visited · Istanbul
Istanbul sits at the literal crossroads of continents — East meets West on a single bridge. The Bosphorus at sunset, the Grand Bazaar. Unforgettable.
